Generated by Claude Opus

This scan identified 2 critical and 6 high-severity findings across 8 hosts on Acme Corp's internal network. The single most material issue is a Redis instance running on the application server (10.0.5.41:6379) with no authentication enabled — it stores session cookies for the customer portal, meaning an attacker on the network can hijack any logged-in user's session in under one minute of access.

A separate Elasticsearch instance on the same host is similarly unauthenticated and indexes 142 GB of customer support tickets containing email addresses, account IDs, and in 3% of cases, plaintext passwords pasted by users. We assess this as the highest-impact finding because it is both currently exploitable and would constitute a reportable breach under both GDPR Article 34 and your standard SOC 2 customer data agreements.

We recommend three immediate actions, in order: (1) set a Redis password and bind to 127.0.0.1 — 5 minutes of work, removes the session-hijacking path; (2) enable X-Pack security on Elasticsearch — 20 minutes, removes the customer-data exposure; (3) rotate the AWS access key found leaked in your internal-tools repo and audit the past 90 days of CloudTrail for unexpected use of that principal.
