Section 01 · Executive summary
01 AI-narrated

Executive summary

Northwind Fintech's external attack surface contains 47 findings across 14 in-scope hosts, including 7 critical exposures that an APT-level adversary could chain into customer database access within an estimated 72 hours of opportunistic discovery. The most consequential single weakness is an unauthenticated Redis instance on cache-edge-02.example.com sitting one network hop away from the payments service, alongside a forgotten internet-facing Windows host still vulnerable to MS17-010 (EternalBlue) and a Jenkins build server reachable from the public internet accepting admin:admin. In aggregate, the perimeter exhibits the signature of a fast-growth fintech where production guardrails have not yet caught up to engineering velocity.

Business impact is concentrated in three areas. First, customer data exposure. A successful pivot through the Jenkins build pipeline grants short-lived AWS credentials with read access to a backups bucket containing 14 months of cardholder-adjacent metadata — a discoverable PCI-DSS scope violation. Second, financial integrity. The Citrix NetScaler appliance at the edge has not received the CVE-2023-3519 patch, an unauthenticated RCE actively exploited by APT29 and several ransomware affiliates; compromise here yields a foothold inside the payments VPC. Third, brand and regulatory. An expired wildcard TLS certificate on the public API is silently downgrading client trust and would be a reportable finding in the next SOC 2 surveillance audit window.

The good news is that mitigation is concentrated. Closing five specific findings — the Redis bind, the Jenkins exposure, the NetScaler patch, the public S3 bucket policy, and the EternalBlue host — collapses the modeled adversary exposure score from 8.4 / 10 to 5.1 / 10 and removes the only end-to-end attack path Xarex was able to construct against a designated crown jewel. We recommend treating these five items as a sprint-zero burndown with a 7-day SLA and assigning each to a named owner before the next continuous scan cycle on 2026-05-21.

Top three immediate actions — (1) Remove public exposure of Redis on cache-edge-02 and rotate any keys it accessed; (2) Patch Citrix NetScaler to firmware 14.1-12.35 or later and audit session tokens issued in the last 60 days; (3) Take the Jenkins controller off the public internet behind a VPN or identity-aware proxy and rotate every pipeline secret.

Critical

Section 08 · Methodology
08 How we scanned

Methodology

Xarex operates as a read-only, non-destructive autonomous scanner. No exploitation is attempted against findings — vulnerability status is established via banner grabs, version fingerprinting, configuration inspection, and default-credential probes only. Where credentials are tested, they are drawn from a list of vendor defaults (e.g. admin:admin, root:root); no brute-force or customer-specific password lists are used. Active denial-of-service tests are never executed.

The scan proceeds through six sequential phases, each composed of independent modules that emit structured evidence consumed by the AI narrator (Claude Sonnet, hosted via Anthropic API; redacted prompts only).

Phase Name Purpose Modules used in this scan
1Host discoveryIdentify live in-scope hosts and DNS surfacedns-enum, tcp-ping, cert-transparency
2Port scanMap open TCP/UDP ports per hostsyn-scan, udp-top-100
3Service fingerprintIdentify software, version, and bannerbanner-grab, http-fp, tls-fp
4Default-credential testSingle attempt per service with vendor-default credscred-probe (admin/admin only)
5Specialized checksCVE confirmation, cloud misconfig, TLS hygienecve-match, s3-perms, tls-audit, smb-check
6AI narration & reportingCluster findings, narrate, map to TTPs, rendernarrate, ttp-map, render-pdf

Scope and limitations

  • External-only. This engagement covers internet-facing assets within the agreed scope. Internal network segments, employee endpoints, and SaaS tenants were not in scope.
  • No exploitation. Findings reflect credible vulnerability, not confirmed compromise. CVE matches are derived from version fingerprinting; in a small number of cases a vendor-supplied patch may have been backported without changing the version string.
  • EPSS percentiles are sourced from FIRST.org's published feeds at scan time. They represent global exploit-likelihood, not Northwind-specific targeting probability.
  • AI narration is a generative summary of the structured findings; it is reviewed for factual consistency against the underlying evidence by an automated grounding check, but the rule-engine output is authoritative when the two disagree.
  • Threat actor mappings are advisory and derived from public MITRE ATT&CK group profiles; they describe TTP overlap, not actual targeting.
