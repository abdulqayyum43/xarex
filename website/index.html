The Problem

Security tools are stuck in 2015.

Existing vulnerability scanners tell you what's broken. They don't tell you how an attacker would exploit it, which assets are most at risk, or what to fix first to save the most money.

💸
Eye-watering cost
Nessus Pro: $3,990/year. Rapid7 InsightVM: $5,000+/year. Manual penetration tests: $15,000–$50,000 per engagement. Enterprise tools require enterprise budgets — even for 50-host networks.
You're paying $332–$4,000+/month for tools Xarex replaces at $49.
📋
No attack context
Traditional scanners list CVEs. They don't model how an adversary would chain those vulnerabilities across your network, which paths lead to your crown jewels, or how real APT groups would attack you specifically.
Without context, you're patching blindly and wasting remediation budget.
🐢
Point-in-time, not continuous
Manual pentests happen once a year. New CVEs are published daily. Infrastructure changes constantly. A finding that wasn't present at your last pentest could be exposing you right now — and you'd never know.
Breaches go undetected for months on average — a new CVE introduced last week won't appear until your next scheduled scan.

FAQ

Common questions

Nessus and Qualys are vulnerability scanners — they find CVEs and report them. Xarex is an autonomous penetration testing platform. It models how an adversary would actually exploit those vulnerabilities, simulates real APT group attack chains, computes blast radius to your critical assets, matches live CVEs to your environment in real-time, and delivers AI-powered executive reports with dollar-ROI for each fix.
Xarex is fully self-hosted. The Cloud Brain runs on your own infrastructure (or a VPS you control). Your scan data, findings, and reports never leave your environment. The only external call is to the NIST NVD API for CVE Watch (read-only, no data sent) and optionally to the Anthropic API for AI report generation (only finding metadata, no IPs or hostnames sent).
On the Starter plan, AI reports use your own Anthropic API key — billed directly by Anthropic at their published rates (typically a few cents to a few dollars per report, depending on finding count). Pro includes a shared Claude Opus quota sufficient for most teams with no additional Anthropic bill. Enterprise gets a dedicated quota. You can always start without an API key — the built-in rules engine generates risk scores, MITRE mapping, and remediation plans without Claude.
